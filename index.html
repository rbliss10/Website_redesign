Mike Medicine Horse

2D and 3D Artist and Animator

My Story

Mike is a Crow Indian from Montana and moved to the Twin Cities for college over 20 years ago. In his free time, he's either drawing, playing fighting games, or playing guitar. He's always told himself "if creating art is no longer an option, I’ll be happy playing guitar in a blues band". Outside of work, he has a loving, understanding wife and 3 kids who happily take up the rest of his time. Mike often considers himself a “pop” artist in the sense that he's most influenced by modern media (film, music, comics) with a firm belief in classical art and foundation. For Mike, it’s about draftsmanship as well as concept. Admittedly, he tends to lean more towards technical execution and less towards ideas, but it’s something he's aware of and works on. For his 3D work, he works mainly in Zbrush, Maya, C4D and Vray. For 2D illustration, his main tools are Manga Studio (Clip Studio) for pencils and Photoshop for colors. When creating art for 2D and 3D, his initial goals are building a solid foundation. He asks himself questions like “Is the form working here?” or “Does the composition work at a basic level?”. One of the important lessons he's learned is to not be too committed to a certain way of working. If he can catch that he's frustrated and a piece isn’t working, he's not above starting over or trying something different.
